# Volume Landmarks — Research References

This document lists the research and sources behind the volume landmarks used in JSON.fit's program generation. The numbers in [volume-landmarks.md](./volume-landmarks.md) are synthesised from the meta-analyses, peer-reviewed studies, and recognised practitioner sources listed below.

All volume landmarks in JSON.fit use **fractional set counting** (Primary × 1.0 + Secondary × 0.5), which is the same convention used in Pelland et al. 2024 and Remmert et al. 2025 — the most current and rigorous meta-regressions on resistance training volume. Their analyses found fractional counting to be the most predictive of hypertrophy outcomes vs. total counting (1.0 for any contribution) or direct-only counting (0 for indirect contribution).

## Primary Meta-Analyses and Meta-Regressions

**Pelland, J. C., Remmert, J. F., Robinson, Z. P., Hinson, S. R., & Zourdos, M. C. (2025).** The Resistance Training Dose Response: Meta-Regressions Exploring the Effects of Weekly Volume and Frequency on Muscle Hypertrophy and Strength Gains. *Sports Medicine.*
PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41343037/
*The most current and rigorous dose-response meta-regression. Uses fractional set counting, which is the convention this document is built on. 67 studies, 2,058 subjects. Key findings: minimum effective dose ~4 fractional sets/week, substantial diminishing returns beyond ~12–20 sets/week, functional plateau at ~31 sets/week.*

**Remmert, J., Pelland, J., Robinson, Z., Hinson, S., & Zourdos, M. (2025).** Is There Too Much of a Good Thing? Meta-Regressions of the Effect of Per-Session Volume on Hypertrophy and Strength. *SportRxiv.*
https://sportrxiv.org/index.php/server/preprint/view/537
*Companion study identifying per-session diminishing returns at ~11 fractional sets for hypertrophy.*

**Schoenfeld, B. J., Ogborn, D., & Krieger, J. W. (2017).** Dose-response relationship between weekly resistance training volume and increases in muscle mass: A systematic review and meta-analysis. *Journal of Sports Sciences, 35*(11), 1073–1082.
PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27433992/
*Foundational dose-response meta-analysis. Established the 10+ sets per week threshold for maximising muscle growth and the ~0.37% per-set hypertrophy benefit.*

**Krieger, J. W. (2010).** Single vs. multiple sets of resistance exercise for muscle hypertrophy: A meta-analysis. *Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research, 24*(4), 1150–1159.
PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20300012/
*Found multiple sets produce ~40% greater hypertrophy than single sets, with dose-response through 4–6 sets per exercise.*

**Baz-Valle, E., Balsalobre-Fernández, C., Alix-Fages, C., & Santos-Concejero, J. (2022).** A Systematic Review of the Effects of Different Resistance Training Volumes on Muscle Hypertrophy. *Journal of Human Kinetics, 81,* 199–210.
PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8884877/
*Meta-analysis comparing moderate vs. high (>20 sets/week) volumes; found high volume more beneficial for triceps but not for quads or biceps over moderate volume.*

**Heaselgrave, S. R., Blacker, J., Smeuninx, B., McKendry, J., & Breen, L. (2019).** Dose-response relationship of weekly resistance-training volume and frequency on muscular adaptations in trained men. *International Journal of Sports Physiology and Performance, 14*(3), 360–368.
PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30160627/
*Found that 27 weekly biceps sets did not produce more hypertrophy than 18 sets, suggesting an inverted-U at very high volumes.*

**Currier, B. S., McLeod, J. C., Banfield, L., et al. (2023).** Resistance training prescription for muscle strength and hypertrophy in healthy adults: a systematic review and Bayesian network meta-analysis. *British Journal of Sports Medicine, 57*(18), 1211–1220.
*Bayesian network meta-analysis covering volume, intensity, and frequency.*

**Grgic, J., Schoenfeld, B. J., Davies, T. B., Lazinica, B., Krieger, J. W., & Pedisic, Z. (2018).** Effect of Resistance Training Frequency on Gains in Muscular Strength: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis. *Sports Medicine, 48,* 1207–1220.

**Robinson, Z. P., Pelland, J. C., Remmert, J. F., Refalo, M. C., Jukic, I., Steele, J., et al. (2024).** Exploring the Dose-Response Relationship Between Estimated Resistance Training Proximity to Failure, Strength Gain, and Muscle Hypertrophy: A Series of Meta-Regressions. *Sports Medicine, 54*(9), 2209–2231.

## Note on Auxiliary Muscles

The following muscles do not have peer-reviewed volume landmark research. Their landmarks in JSON.fit are practitioner-derived estimates extrapolated from analogous small-muscle conventions and coaching consensus:

- Neck
- Obliques
- Hip Abductors
- Hip Adductors
- Serratus Anterior
- Shins (Tibialis Anterior)

Treat these values as approximate. They will be updated if and when peer-reviewed research becomes available.
