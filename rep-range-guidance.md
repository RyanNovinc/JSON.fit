# Rep Range Guidance

Research-backed guidance for assigning rep ranges by exercise category and training goal. Apply the appropriate guidance based on the user's goal and exercise selection.

The dominant principle: rep range is largely a logistical choice for hypertrophy (5–30 reps work similarly when sets are taken close to failure and weekly sets are equated). Strength training has a real but modest preference for heavy loads (≥80% 1RM) due to specificity. Map rep ranges to exercise category, not abstract goal labels.

---

## Default Mapping by Exercise Category

The bands below balance research, joint stress, technique tolerance, and stimulus-to-fatigue per exercise type.

### Heavy compound barbell lifts
Back squat, conventional/sumo deadlift, bench press, overhead press, weighted pull-up, weighted dip.

- **Hypertrophy:** 4–10 reps. Floor of 4 limits per-rep joint cost. Ceiling of 10 because higher reps on barbell squats and deadlifts compromise technique and stimulus-to-fatigue ratio.
- **Strength:** 1–6 reps at ≥80% 1RM. 3–5 sets, 2–5 min rest. RPE 7–9 most sets.

### Moderate compound lifts
Machine chest press, Hammer Strength rows, T-bar/cable rows, lat pulldown, leg press, hack squat, pendulum squat.

- **Hypertrophy:** 6–15 reps. The "sweet spot" — stable load, low technique-failure risk, allows pushing closer to failure than barbell compounds.
- **Strength:** 4–8 reps as primary stimulus; 6–10 as accessories.

### Unilateral compound lifts
Bulgarian split squat, walking lunge, single-leg press, single-arm DB row, step-up.

- **Hypertrophy:** 6–15 reps per side. Balance and stability cap useful low-rep work.
- **Strength:** 5–10 reps per side. Pure-strength single-leg work is rare in research.

### Large-muscle isolation
RDL/SLDL (when treated as hamstring isolation), leg extension, lying/seated leg curl, pec deck, machine reverse fly, hip thrust (when treated as glute-isolated).

- **Hypertrophy:** 8–20 reps. Load is mechanically simple and machines forgive higher reps.
- **Strength:** Not a primary strength target. If loaded heavily for accessory strength, 6–10 reps with maintained technique.

### Small-muscle isolation
DB/cable biceps curls, triceps extensions, lateral raises, calf raises, rear delt fly, wrist curls.

- **Hypertrophy:** 8–20 reps for free-weight isolation; up to 25–30 reps for pump-style cable and machine work.
- **Strength:** Not a primary strength target. Heavy curls or skull crushers at 6–8 reps are reasonable for those without joint issues but offer no clear advantage over moderate-rep work.

### Power/explosive movements
Power clean, hang clean, snatch, jump squat, push press, medicine ball throws.

- Velocity-loaded, not rep-loaded. Typical loading: 30–60% 1RM for "speed-strength," 60–85% for "strength-speed." Reps per set kept low (1–5) to maintain bar speed. Sets terminated by velocity loss (commonly 10–20% loss) rather than rep count.
- Do NOT count these in hypertrophy volume.

## Goal-Specific Adjustments

### Hypertrophy
- Use any rep range from ~5 to ~30 within the category constraints above
- Distribute opportunistically: heavier on stable, well-tolerated compounds; moderate on machine compounds; higher on isolation
- **Volume is the primary dose variable** (~10–20 fractional sets per muscle per week, building toward upper end across mesocycle)
- **Proximity to failure is the key effort variable** — most sets at 1–3 RIR
- Below 5 reps: hypertrophy still occurs but per-set returns diminish, joint stress accumulates
- Above 30 reps: equivalence breaks down; light loads require closer proximity to failure to "work"

### Maximum Strength
- Heavy primary lifts: 1–5 reps, ~80–95% 1RM, on competition movements
- Moderate-load accessory work: 5–10 reps
- Isolation for connective tissue and weak-point hypertrophy: 8–15 reps
- Volume requirements plateau quickly — ~3–6 hard sets per powerlift per week is enough for trained powerlifters
- Strength is largely RIR-insensitive across 0–4 RIR; leaving reps in tank costs little, buys recovery

### Body Recomposition / Fat Loss
- **Do not change rep ranges for fat loss.** Rep range itself does not meaningfully affect fat loss when calorie deficit is matched.
- Maintain the same hypertrophy-oriented training plan
- Expect proximity-to-failure tolerance to drop ~1 RIR
- Expect ~5–10% performance lag in late-deficit phases
- The "high reps for tone" claim is not supported

### General Fitness
- Any rep range from 6–20 with adequate volume, frequency, and proximity to failure works
- Choose for adherence, joint comfort, and personal preference

### Sport-Specific
- Specificity dominates: power athletes use velocity-based loading, strength athletes train near 1RM, field-sport athletes train both heavy compounds and explosive work alongside high-rep accessories
- Layer power/velocity-loaded movements over a base of strength and hypertrophy work

## Within-Mesocycle Progression

### Default: Double Progression
Work to top of rep range with current load, add load when ceiling reached, return to bottom of range. Well supported across the literature. Consistent with volume-equivalence finding because effort per set stays high while loads increase.

**Trigger to abandon double progression:** Two sessions of stalls with no change in RIR.

### When to Adjust
- **Switch to load-only progression** if rep range bottom is being exceeded routinely
- **Switch to rep-only progression** if joints are flaring under added load

### Periodization Variants
- Linear vs. daily undulating periodization: trivial difference for hypertrophy (Cohen's d ≈ −0.02)
- Pyramid vs. straight sets: no clinically meaningful between-group differences
- Block periodization with rep range shifts (12s → 8s → 5s): supported by tradition more than direct trials

Periodization variants matter less than total volume, frequency, proximity to failure, and load specificity for the goal.

## Operating Principles

### Volume × Effort > Rep Range
For hypertrophy: target ~10–20 fractional sets per muscle group per week. Sets to 0–2 RIR contribute roughly the same hypertrophic stimulus regardless of whether they're 6, 12, or 25 reps. Sets at 4+ RIR contribute meaningfully less per set.

### Strength Has Different Logic
Volume requirements plateau quickly (~3 hard sets per powerlift per week). Heavy load specificity matters. Proximity to failure barely matters — leaving 1–3 reps in the tank is essentially as effective as grinding for strength gains.

### Map by Category, Not Goal
A "hypertrophy program" doesn't need everything in 8–12. A heavy compound at 6 reps and an isolation at 18 reps can both be hypertrophy work. Each exercise gets the rep range that maximises its specific stimulus-to-fatigue ratio.

### Adjust for Individual
- **Joint pain in any lift** → remove from low-rep zone immediately; pull isolation into 12–25 range
- **1RM testing within 8 weeks** → shift primary lifts to 1–5 reps for at least 3–4 weeks for specificity
- **Stagnation across complete mesocycle** → audit weekly fractional sets, proximity to failure, and total hard set count BEFORE changing rep range
- **Inability to recover between sessions** → drop volume by 30%, hold rep range, before changing rep range

## Common Misconceptions to Avoid

### "Compound 6–12, Isolation 10–15" is a Rule
It's a defensible default, not a research finding. The exact numbers are habit. The general band is reasonable but should flex with exercise category and individual joint tolerance.

### "Isolation Muscles Respond Better to Higher Reps"
Probably false. They respond similarly to load and effort. Higher reps for isolation are a workaround for joint stress, technical fragility, and progressive overload practicality — not a biological preference.

### "Different Rep Ranges for Fat Loss"
Not supported. Rep range does not meaningfully affect fat loss when calorie deficit is matched.

### "Metabolic Stress / The Pump Drives Hypertrophy"
Evidence has shifted toward mechanical tension and effective volume as principal drivers. The pump correlates with proximity to failure and may be a marker rather than a separable mechanism.

### "Strict 8–12 is the Hypertrophy Zone"
The 8–12 zone is not biologically privileged. Hypertrophy is similar across roughly 30–85% 1RM (~5–30 reps) when sets are equated and taken close to failure. Use 8–12 if it suits the exercise, not because it's "the hypertrophy range."

## Stimulating Reps / Fractional Set Framework

The "stimulating reps" concept (popularised by Beardsley) suggests only the last ~5 reps of a set to failure are highly stimulating. Mechanistically suggestive but not strongly supported as a sharp cutoff. Empirical pattern matches a continuous proximity-to-failure relationship rather than a hard threshold.

The Pelland 2024/2026 fractional-set framework formalises volume counting: direct sets count fully (1.0), indirect sets count at half (0.5). Bayes Factor of 9.48 in favor of fractional over total counting — strong evidence.

Practical application:
- Count direct sets fully (the measured muscle is the prime mover)
- Count indirect sets at half (synergist contribution: e.g., barbell row contributes 1 to back, 0.5 to biceps)
- Sets to ~0–2 RIR contribute roughly the same hypertrophic volume regardless of rep count
- Sets at 4+ RIR contribute meaningfully less per set

## Honest Limits

- "Effort-matched" is hard to operationalize; RIR predictions reliable to within ~1 rep on average; accuracy worse far from failure
- Most literature uses untrained or recreationally trained subjects over 6–12 weeks; long-term advanced trainee data is limited
- Whether very heavy loads (1–3 RM) produce equivalent hypertrophy with only sets matched is unclear
- Whether moderate loads enjoy a small genuine hypertrophy edge over very heavy loads when sets-but-not-volume are matched is plausible but unconfirmed
- Trained-population data is thinner than untrained; magnitude of effects in elite trainees may differ
- Individual variation is large; cited effect sizes are population means; meta-analytic recommendations are starting defaults to be calibrated by tracked response
- The Pelland 2024/2026 fractional-set framework is recent and not yet replicated by an independent group
