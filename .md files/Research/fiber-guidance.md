# Fiber Intake Guidance

Research-backed guidance for daily fiber intake, scaled by user goal, calorie level, and tolerance.

The default formula scales fiber with caloric intake (14 g per 1,000 kcal), but with practical caps and floors based on goal-specific evidence. Apply the appropriate guidance based on the user's training and nutrition goal.

---

## Default Formula

**14 g of fiber per 1,000 kcal of planned intake**, with these adjustments:
- **Floor:** 25 g/day for women, 30 g/day for men (NHMRC AI; EFSA; WHO)
- **Soft ceiling:** ~45 g/day for high-calorie diets (practical GI tolerance limit)
- **Distribution:** ~7 g per main meal, ~3 g per snack
- **Source priority:** whole foods first, supplements only as adjuncts

---

## Goal-Specific Recommendations

### Bulking / Muscle Building (≥3,500 kcal/day)

- **Target: 35–45 g/day** — do NOT blindly scale to 56–63 g/day at 14 g per 1,000 kcal
- **Why the cap:** Pre-workout GI distress, gas/bloating, early satiety competing with caloric intake
- Use refined-carb portions (white rice, white potato, fruit juice, bread) to fill calories without GI volume penalty
- **Pre-training meal ≤5 g fiber** in the 2–3 hours before resistance training
- Drop fiber 5 g/day at a time toward 30 g/day floor if user reports persistent bloating or fails to hit caloric targets
- Include ~2 g/day β-glucan (40–50 g oats supplies most of this)

### Fat Loss / Caloric Deficit

- **Preserve absolute fiber grams — do NOT scale down with calories**
- **Target: 30–38 g/day** even at 1,500–1,800 kcal intake
- Bias toward viscous fibers (psyllium, β-glucan, glucomannan, legumes) — strongest satiety evidence
- Consider 5–10 g psyllium 15 minutes before the largest meal
- High-volume vegetables become cornerstone (broccoli, leafy greens, cauliflower)
- Watch for paradox: extreme deficits with very high fiber can crowd protein — cap fiber if necessary

### Body Recomposition

- **Target: 30–35 g/day baseline**
- Raise to 35–40 g on higher-calorie training days
- Distribute and time as for fat loss

### General Health / Maintenance

- **Target: 30–38 g/day** for most adults
- Achievable with: 50 g oats breakfast (~5 g) + 2 fruit servings (~6 g) + 2 vegetable servings (~8 g) + 1 legume serving (~7 g) + 2 wholegrain bread slices (~6 g) ≈ 32 g

---

## Per-Meal Distribution

Apply the Mancin et al. 2025 distribution pattern:
- ~7 g per main meal
- ~3 g per snack

This typically yields 25–35 g/day across 3–5 eating occasions.

---

## Source Priority

### Whole Foods (foundation — use first)
- **Legumes:** 7–8 g per ½ cup cooked (lentils, black beans, chickpeas)
- **Oats:** ~4 g per 40 g serving (also supplies β-glucan)
- **Berries:** raspberries 8 g/cup, blackberries similar
- **Vegetables:** broccoli 5 g/cup, leafy greens, Brussels sprouts
- **Whole grains:** wholegrain bread 2–3 g/slice, brown rice, quinoa
- **Fruit with skin:** pear 5 g, apple 4 g
- **Nuts/seeds:** chia 4 g/tbsp, ground flax 2 g/tbsp, almonds 12.5 g/100 g

### Supplements (use as adjuncts only)
- **Psyllium:** most clinically validated. 5–10 g 1–3× daily before meals. Best for satiety, LDL, glycemia.
- **β-glucan (oat/barley):** ≥3 g/day for cholesterol benefit (FDA/EFSA claim threshold)
- **Inulin/FOS:** potent prebiotic but high gas burden — tolerance ~10 g/day
- **Methylcellulose:** synthetic, viscous, no gas — useful for laxation only

### What NOT to Optimize For
- **Soluble vs insoluble ratios:** No guideline endorses specific ratios. Modern research (McRorie & McKeown 2017) suggests viscosity and fermentability matter more than the soluble/insoluble dichotomy
- **Specific microbiome targeting:** Mechanistic evidence is strong, but human RCT translation to hard outcomes is limited. Don't over-claim "feeding the microbiome"

A useful diversity heuristic: **aim for 30 different plant foods per week** (American Gut Project pattern) rather than tracking ratios.

## Special Considerations

### Pre-Training (within 2–3 hours)
**Keep fiber LOW — under 5 g.** Pre-workout fiber delays gastric emptying and increases intra-abdominal bulk; established cause of GI distress during exercise. ACSM 2016 position stand explicitly recommends low-fiber pre-training meals.

### Carb Loading / Weight Cutting
**<10 g/day for 4 days** during fight week or carb-loading window (per ISSN combat sports position stand 2025). Not routine practice — only for explicit cutting protocols.

### Building Tolerance When Starting Low
For users currently <20 g/day starting a new plan:
- **Ramp ~5 g/day every 1–2 weeks** (4-week ramp + 2-week maintenance per Mancin 2025)
- Never increase more than 10 g/day above habitual intake
- Adequate fluid intake (≥2 L/day) is essential

### FODMAP Sensitivity
Some users experience symptoms attributed to "high fiber" that are actually FODMAP issues. Common FODMAP triggers:
- Onion, garlic
- Wheat (in some people)
- Beans, lentils
- Certain fruits (apples, pears, mangoes)

**Low-FODMAP swaps that preserve fiber:** oats, kiwifruit, carrots, berries, rice, quinoa, firm bananas. Substitution often resolves symptoms while maintaining fiber target.

### Elevated LDL or Pre-Diabetes
- Add **5–10 g/day psyllium** in divided doses before meals
- Expected effect: LDL −0.33 mmol/L (~−13 mg/dL)
- Ensure ≥3 g/day β-glucan (cholesterol claim threshold)

### Active GI Disease
Users with IBD, IBS flares, or diverticulitis flares may need lower-fiber or FODMAP-modified strategies. The Reynolds 2019 evidence base is in healthy adults; do NOT default to high fiber for symptomatic GI users.

## Tolerance Thresholds

There is **no formal Upper Intake Level** for fiber. Symptom emergence is highly individual:

- **<25 g/day:** typically symptom-free in adults already eating moderate fiber
- **25–40 g/day:** the guideline range; symptom-free for most when introduced gradually
- **~50 g/day:** gas, bloating, altered bowel habits begin appearing in many individuals
- **~70 g/day:** symptoms become common and pronounced; potential mineral malabsorption

Specific fiber tolerance ceilings:
- Inulin/FOS: ~10 g/day
- GOS: ~12 g/day
- Psyllium: up to ~30 g/day
- Resistant maltodextrin: ~40 g/day

---

## When to Change the Default

**Reduce fiber if:**
- User reports >2 weeks of persistent bloating, abdominal pain, or altered bowel habits
- User cannot hit caloric targets on a bulk specifically due to early satiety
- User is in active GI disease flare

**Increase fiber if:**
- User reports constipation
- User has elevated LDL → add psyllium, hit β-glucan target
- User has pre-diabetes → upper end of guideline range with viscous fibers

**Drop to <10 g/day temporarily if:**
- Carb loading for endurance event
- Cutting weight for combat sports (4 days max)

---

## Operating Principle

Fiber is one of the highest-leverage levers in a fat-loss diet (satiety, glycemia, cholesterol) and a real practical concern in a bulk (gastric volume, GI distress). The 14 g per 1,000 kcal default works for most users at moderate caloric intakes but should be capped at high calories and preserved at low calories.

The dominant adaptation drivers remain:
1. Total caloric intake matching the goal
2. Total daily protein (for muscle outcomes)
3. Whole-food source quality and diversity
4. Adherence and sustainability

Fiber optimization is a meaningful secondary lever — particularly valuable for users with cardiovascular concerns, glycemic issues, or fat-loss satiety challenges.
