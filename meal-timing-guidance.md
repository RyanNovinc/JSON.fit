# Meal Timing Guidance for Sleep Quality and Circadian Health

Research-backed guidance for meal timing relative to sleep, organized by user-selected optimization tier.

The user's tier comes from their questionnaire selection: Minimal, Moderate, or Maximum. Apply the appropriate column based on their selection.

---

## Per-Tier Recommendation Matrix

| Tier | Last meal before bed | First meal after waking | Total eating window |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Minimal** (basic sleep hygiene) | ≥2 h | Within 2 h | ≤14 h |
| **Moderate** (enhanced sleep) | ≥3 h | Within 1–2 h | ~12 h |
| **Maximum** (optimal sleep & circadian) | ≥3 h (4 h is conservative target) | Within 1 h, with substantive breakfast | ~10 h, weighted earlier in the day |

**Confidence levels:**
- 2 h buffer (Minimal): Moderate confidence — supported by reflux RCT and large-sample observational data
- 3 h buffer (Moderate): Moderate-high confidence — multiple converging evidence streams
- 4 h buffer (Maximum): Practitioner extrapolation, not RCT-validated as superior to 3 h
- First meal within 1 h: Practitioner extrapolation; "morning eating" is supported, exact cutoff is not
- 12 h vs 10 h windows: Practitioner extrapolation; head-to-head comparisons don't exist

## Goal-Specific Adjustments

### Hypertrophy / Muscle Building
Apply the user's selected tier for full-meal timing, but a 20–40 g casein dose ~30 min before bed is supported and does NOT conflict with the buffer rule. The pre-sleep casein literature uses small (~140–500 kcal) liquid boluses, which empty quickly and have minimal reflux risk. This is categorically different from a full meal close to bed. Total daily protein ≥1.6 g/kg with even distribution; ≥0.4 g/kg per meal recommended.

### Fat Loss
Apply the user's selected tier. Time-restricted eating is not superior to caloric restriction alone over 12 months — choose the easier-to-comply approach. If using TRE, ensure protein adequacy (≥1.6 g/kg) to avoid disproportionate lean mass loss. The TREAT trial showed ~65% of weight lost was lean mass when protein wasn't controlled — roughly 2.5× the typical lean-mass fraction.

### Body Recomposition
Use Moderate tier framework with adequate protein (≥1.6 g/kg/day, 0.4 g/kg per meal) and resistance training. Pre-sleep casein 20–40 g is supported. Window narrowing should not compromise total protein intake.

### Type 2 Diabetes / Metabolic Syndrome / Prediabetes
Front-loaded eating with biggest meal at breakfast. 8–10 h window with dinner before 6–7 PM. This is the population with the strongest RCT support for early TRE.

### Athletes with Evening Training
Drop the 3 h buffer for training-meal proximity — peri-workout nutrition outweighs eating-window concerns. Pre-sleep casein 20–40 g is recommended after evening resistance training.

### General Health
Moderate tier recommendations are well-tolerated and supported by the strongest signals (reflux, WASO, circadian alignment).

## Special Considerations

### Pre-Sleep Protein Reconciliation
A 20–40 g casein bolus 30 min before bed does NOT violate the meal buffer rule. Critical distinction:
- A small (≤500 kcal) lean liquid casein dose: minimal gastric load, minimal reflux risk, increases overnight muscle protein synthesis ~22%
- A full mixed meal close to bed: substantial gastric load, increased reflux risk, sleep architecture disruption

The buffer rule applies to FULL MEALS. Pre-sleep casein protocols are compatible with all three tiers.

### Caffeine Timing
Avoid caffeine for at least 6 hours before planned bedtime; 8 hours is more conservative for sensitive users. Even 6 hours before bed, 400 mg caffeine reduces objective sleep time by more than one hour. Caffeine half-life varies considerably by individual (CYP1A2 genotype).

### Composition Matters
A small, lean, protein-skewed evening meal (400–500 kcal, ≤30% fat) is much less disruptive than a large, fatty, late dinner of equal kcal. High-fat meals delay gastric emptying and worsen reflux. The buffer is a heuristic, not a biological law — composition × timing interact.

### Shift Workers
Standard tiers don't apply. Use a small night snack (~10% of daily energy) rather than a full meal during the biological night. The buffer rule applies to whatever sleep period the worker actually gets. Total dietary inversion has metabolic costs that aren't fully avoidable.

### When to Tighten the Buffer
- User reports nocturnal reflux → tighten last-meal buffer to ≥4 h regardless of tier; reduce evening fat
- User has prediabetes or T2D → escalate to early-TRE pattern (10 h window ending by 6 PM) regardless of weight goal

### Eating Disorder Considerations
Users with active or historical eating disorders should NOT be steered toward narrowed eating windows. Apply Minimal tier framing only.

## Operating Principle

The strongest evidence supports avoiding full meals within 1–3 hours of bed. Beyond that, returns diminish. Effect sizes for sleep architecture and overnight metabolism are statistically robust but modest — meal timing is a meaningful lever but not a primary determinant of body composition or health.

The dominant nutritional drivers remain:
1. Total daily energy balance
2. Protein adequacy and distribution
3. Fiber and food quality
4. Adherence and sustainability

Meal timing optimization within evidence-supported ranges is a smaller-magnitude lever than these. Sleep optimization through meal timing is most valuable for users with reflux, insulin resistance, or actual sleep complaints.
