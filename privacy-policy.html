Privacy Policy

🔒 Your workouts, meals and health data stay on your device.
Last Updated: June 2026

The short version

🛡️

Your fitness and nutrition data stays on your device. JSON.fit has no accounts and never uploads your workouts, meal plans or health information. To understand how the app and website are used and to make them better, we collect anonymous, non-identifying usage analytics. We don't show ads and we never sell your data.

This policy explains, in plain English, exactly what that means for the JSON.fit iOS and Android apps and for the JSON.fit website (json.fit).

1. Who we are

JSON.fit is built and operated by Ryan Novinc (a sole trader based in Australia). In this policy, "JSON.fit", "we", "us" and "our" refer to that operation. "App" means the JSON.fit mobile app on the App Store and Google Play. "Website" means json.fit and its pages.

If you have any privacy question, you can reach us any time at support@json.fit.

2. Data that stays on your device (and is never collected by us)

The app is designed to keep your personal fitness and nutrition information local to your device. We do not receive it, store it on our servers, or send it anywhere. Because this data is processed only on your device, it is not "collected" by us. This includes:

Fitness & workout data

  • Imported workout routines and training programs
  • Exercise progress, logs and workout calendar data
  • Personal notes and customisations

Nutrition & health data

  • Nutrition goals and macro targets
  • Health conditions, dietary restrictions and food preferences
  • Weight tracking history and measurements
  • Meal plans, recipes and cooking instructions
  • Food inventory, grocery lists, and any budget or location info you enter for pricing
  • Supplement intake, meal timing and meal-prep sessions

App preferences

  • App settings and preferences

This information may be included in your device's own backups (for example iCloud or Google backups). Those backups are managed entirely by Apple or Google under their privacy policies — we have no access to them.

3. Data we do collect

a. Anonymous usage & diagnostics analytics

To understand how JSON.fit is actually used — which features help, where people get stuck, whether something is broken — the app and website collect anonymous, non-identifying analytics. This is the only meaningful data we collect about how you use JSON.fit. It typically includes:

  • App and website interactions and events (for example screens viewed, features used, and actions like importing a plan or program)
  • General device and technical information (device type, operating system, app version, browser type, language)
  • An approximate region derived from your IP address (for example country or city level)
  • Diagnostic and crash information that helps us fix problems

This analytics data is not linked to your identity. We do not attach your name, email, or your on-device workout/nutrition data to it, and we do not use it to build an advertising profile of you or to track you across other companies' apps or websites.

We use the following analytics providers, who process this data on our behalf:

  • Google Analytics (Google LLC) — to measure usage of the app and website. See How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services.
  • Microsoft Clarity (Microsoft Corporation) — for behavioural analytics, including heatmaps and session replays of how the interface is used, with sensitive content masked by default. See the Microsoft Privacy Statement.
  • Our own first-party analytics, hosted on Amazon Web Services (AWS), which records the same kinds of anonymous events.

b. Email address (website newsletter / free cookbook)

If you choose to enter your email address on the website to receive the free cookbook and updates, we collect that email so we can send it to you. This is the only situation in which we collect a personal identifier, and it is entirely optional — you never have to provide it to use the app. Email subscriptions are handled through our email infrastructure on Amazon Web Services (AWS). You can unsubscribe at any time using the link in any email we send, or by emailing support@json.fit.

c. Purchases

JSON.fit is free. There are no subscriptions and no in-app purchases, so we do not process or store any payment information.

4. What we never do

  • We don't show ads.
  • We don't sell, rent or trade your data.
  • We don't build advertising profiles or track you across other companies' apps and websites.
  • We don't put personal information (such as your name or email) into our analytics.
  • We don't upload your on-device workout, nutrition or health data.
  • We don't require an account.

5. Cookies, identifiers & your choices

To make analytics work, the website uses cookies and similar technologies, and the app's analytics tools use device-level identifiers. These are used only for the anonymous analytics described above, never for advertising.

You can limit or switch off this analytics at any time:

  • On the web: install the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on, or block/clear cookies in your browser.
  • On mobile: reset or limit your device's advertising identifier in your phone's privacy settings.

If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom or Switzerland, non-essential analytics and cookies are only intended to run where this is permitted under local law. If you would prefer not to be measured at all, you can use the opt-out options above or simply not use the website.

6. Sharing and third parties

We only share data with the service providers we use to run JSON.fit, and only so they can perform their service for us:

  • Google (Google Analytics)
  • Microsoft (Microsoft Clarity)
  • Amazon Web Services (hosting for our own analytics and email)

We may also disclose information where we are legally required to do so (for example in response to a valid legal request), or to protect the rights, safety or property of JSON.fit, our users or the public. We do not otherwise share your data.

7. International data transfers

Our analytics and email providers (Google, Microsoft and AWS) may process data in the United States and other countries. Where data is transferred internationally, these providers maintain their own legal safeguards for that transfer (for example, certification under the EU–US Data Privacy Framework). Your on-device workout, nutrition and health data does not leave your device, so it is not transferred internationally by us.

8. Data retention

Anonymous analytics data is retained only for limited periods, in line with each provider's settings (for example, Microsoft Clarity retains data for up to 13 months, and Google Analytics retention is time-limited). If you subscribe to the cookbook/newsletter, we keep your email until you unsubscribe. Data stored on your device remains there until you delete it or uninstall the app.

9. Your privacy rights

You can ask us to access, correct or delete personal information we hold about you, and to object to or restrict how we use it. In practice the only personal information we hold is your email address (if you subscribed) — your fitness and nutrition data is on your device, under your control, and you can delete it directly in the app at any time.

To make a request, email support@json.fit. Depending on where you live (for example the EEA, the UK, or California), you may have additional rights under laws such as the GDPR, UK GDPR or CCPA/CPRA; we will honour those rights where they apply. We won't charge you for making a request or treat you differently for exercising your rights.

10. Children's privacy

JSON.fit is intended for users aged 13 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13, and our analytics is not directed at children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information (for example, by subscribing with an email address), contact support@json.fit and we will delete it.

11. Security

Data on your device is protected by your device's own security (such as your passcode and biometric lock), so keeping your device updated and secured protects that data. Analytics data we collect is transmitted over encrypted (HTTPS/TLS) connections.

Important: JSON.fit is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Always consult a healthcare professional for medical, nutrition or dietary decisions, especially if you have a health condition.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we'll change the "Last Updated" date above, and significant changes will be communicated through the app or website.

13. Contact

Questions about this policy or your data? Email us at support@json.fit.
